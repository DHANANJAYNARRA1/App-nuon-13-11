## Core Nursing Symbols Integrated

### üèÆ **1. Authentic Florence Nightingale Oil Lamp**

**Position:** Top center of the logo  
**Design:** Historically accurate oil lamp with handle, round reservoir, spout, wick, and flame  
**Colors:** Purple gradient body, brown wick, orange/yellow flame  
**Animation:** Gentle flickering flame

#### Historical Significance
- Florence Nightingale (1820-1910) is the founder of modern nursing
- Known as "The Lady with the Lamp" during the Crimean War (1854-1856)
- Would make rounds at night carrying an oil lamp to check on wounded soldiers
- The lamp became the universal symbol of nursing care and dedication
- Oil lamps were the standard lighting of the 19th century

#### Authentic Design Elements in Our Logo

**Handle (Left Side):** 
- Curved metal handle for carrying the lamp
- Represents portability - nurses bringing care wherever needed

**Round Oil Reservoir (Main Body):**
- Traditional round shape of 19th-century oil lamps
- Contains the "fuel" - symbolizing nursing knowledge and compassion
- Purple gradient representing wisdom and professionalism

**Spout (Right Side):**
- Extended spout where the wick emerges
- Functional detail showing authentic lamp design

**Wick (Dark Brown):**
- Small rectangular wick emerging from spout
- Draws up the oil to feed the flame
- Represents the conduit between knowledge and practice

**Flame (Orange & Yellow):**
- Realistic teardrop flame shape
- Warm orange outer flame with bright yellow core
- Animated gentle flicker
- Symbolizes:
  - The eternal light of care
  - Knowledge and enlightenment
  - Hope and healing in darkness
  - Professional dedication
  - Continuous learning
